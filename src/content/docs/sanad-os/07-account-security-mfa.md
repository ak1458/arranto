# Account Security & Two-Factor Authentication

SANAD OS supports Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) using any standard authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, Authy, etc.) — a real, second layer of protection on top of your password.

## Enabling 2FA

1. Click **Settings** in the sidebar.
2. Under **Two-Factor Authentication**, click **Enable Two-Factor Auth**.
3. A QR code appears. Open your authenticator app and scan it (or enter the text code shown below the QR code manually if you can't scan).
4. Your app now shows a rotating 6-digit code. Enter it into the field on screen.
5. Click **Confirm & Enable**.

Once enabled, the panel shows **Enabled** with a green checkmark.

## Signing in with 2FA active

After entering your password on future sign-ins, you'll be asked for your 6-digit authenticator code on a separate screen before you reach your dashboard. Enter the current code from your app and click **Verify**.

If you enter an incorrect code, you'll see an error and can try again — there's also a sign-out option on that screen if you need to start over.

## Disabling 2FA

Return to **Settings** and click **Disable** next to your active factor.

## A note on lost devices

If you lose access to your authenticator app, you won't be able to complete sign-in through the normal 2FA challenge screen. Recovery codes for this scenario are on the roadmap but not yet available — use the **"Need help? Contact support"** link on the login page to reach arranto support directly for account recovery.
