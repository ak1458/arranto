# Welcome to SANAD OS

## What SANAD OS is

**سند (SANAD OS)**, by **arranto**, is a maintenance operations and e-invoicing platform built specifically for Saudi contractors who run Annual Maintenance Contracts (AMC) — HVAC, electrical, plumbing, elevators, fire safety, security, IT support, cleaning, and similar field-service trades.

It replaces the spreadsheet-and-WhatsApp way of managing customers, contracts, and invoices with one system that keeps your customer registry, your maintenance contracts, your scheduled visits, and your ZATCA-compliant invoices all in the same place — in Arabic and English.

## Who it's for

Small-to-mid-size Saudi maintenance contractors, typically 10–50 technicians, who currently juggle:

- Customer and site information across spreadsheets
- AMC contract terms, renewal dates, and visit schedules manually tracked
- ZATCA e-invoicing handled separately (or not at all)

If that's you, SANAD OS gives you one login for all of it.

## Arabic-first, fully bilingual

SANAD OS defaults to Arabic with full right-to-left layout, and every screen has complete English support. Switch languages at any time from the globe icon in the top header — nothing is lost, your data displays the same way regardless of which language you're viewing it in.

## The core workflow

Everything in SANAD OS is built around one chain:

**Customer → Site → Asset → Contract → Scheduled Visits → ZATCA-Cleared Invoice**

- A **Customer** is the business you serve (e.g., a bank, a retail chain, a factory).
- A **Site** is one of that customer's physical locations (a branch, a warehouse, a rooftop).
- An **Asset** is a specific piece of equipment at that site (an AC unit, an elevator, a fire panel) that you maintain.
- A **Contract** is the AMC agreement — value, dates, billing terms, and the service lines that define what gets serviced, how often.
- **Visits** are automatically generated from your contract's service frequency (weekly, monthly, quarterly, etc.).
- An **Invoice** is created against a customer and, once you're connected to ZATCA, cryptographically signed and submitted for clearance or reporting — a real, government-compliant e-invoice, not a PDF you print and hope is right.

The rest of this guide walks through each part of that chain, in order.
