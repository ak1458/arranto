# Arranto — Brand Strategy v3

## The correction
v1/v2 were still writing like an agency trying to earn trust through explanation — "founder-led, AI-augmented, direct access, faster iteration" is reassurance copy. Premium brands don't reassure; they assume the visitor already knows what good looks like and simply demonstrate it. This version resets the voice rules around that.

## Reference standard
Apple, Rolex, Linear, Vercel — minimal copy, generous whitespace, motion and craft doing the work text usually does. None of these companies' homepages explain their process, list their tech stack in prose, or justify why the buyer should trust them. They show one thing, precisely, and let it speak.

## Voice rules
- **Say less.** If a sentence can be cut without losing meaning, cut it. A headline plus a single supporting fragment beats a headline plus two sentences of justification.
- **Show, don't argue.** "Founder-led" is a claim; a page that visibly moves fast, loads instantly, and has zero filler *is* founder-led — no need to say it. Where a fact genuinely needs stating (e.g., "since 2017"), state it once, plainly, and move on.
- **No stacked adjectives.** "AI-augmented, founder-led, fast-moving" is three claims doing the work of none. Pick the single strongest true thing per section.
- **No self-description as a category.** Never write "we are a premium AI software studio" on the site itself — that's a sentence for internal strategy docs, not for visitors. A premium brand doesn't announce its own premium-ness; it just is that, visibly.
- **One idea per page.** Homepage: capability. `/work`: proof. `/studio`: the founder. `/contact`: a way in. Don't let any one idea (a product name, a region, a value prop) repeat across sections — repetition on a premium site reads as insecurity, not emphasis.

## Visual/verbal system
Palette and typography stay as already finalized (Ink / Signal Gold / Paper / Muted, Fraunces + Noto Serif Arabic). Dark-only remains correct and now does more of the brand's talking — a confident, quiet visual register replacing the explanatory copy that's being cut.

## The "About"/Studio page — rewritten to the new standard
> Arranto — founded 2017, Gonda.
> Software studio. One engineer. AI-native by design.
> 10+ delivered projects. 118 verified reviews.

That's the entire block. Everything else v1/v2 wrote about "why solo, why AI-augmented, why this beats an agency" is true, useful internally, and completely absent from the public page — it belongs in a founder conversation, not a paragraph a stranger skims past.

## What NOT to do (updated)
- Don't explain why the model (solo, AI-augmented) is good — a visitor who cares will ask; the site doesn't need to pre-argue the case.
- Don't name a region as the audience anywhere on brand-layer pages.
- Don't repeat a product name, claim, or value proposition more than once per page.
- Don't use category-defining language ("we are X") — demonstrate X instead.

## Caveats
- This is a real shift from v1/v2's more explanatory, justification-heavy copy — if any of that reassurance language is still needed somewhere (e.g., a design-partner pitch email, which is a different, private surface), it stays there, not on the public site. See Positioning Strategy v3's brand-layer/proof-layer/outreach-layer split.
