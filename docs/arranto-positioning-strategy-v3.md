# Arranto — Positioning Strategy v3

## The correction
v2 made a real error: it let go-to-market tactics leak into brand identity. ZATCA Compliance Engine, SanadOS, and TehsilOS are real products being built now, and Saudi Arabia is where outreach starts — but "Gulf-focused compliance studio" is a sales motion, not what Arranto *is*. A European, US, or Indian visitor landing on the site should never read it and think "this company isn't for me." Nike doesn't put running shoes in its global tagline even though shoes are most of its revenue. Same principle here.

## What Arranto is, brand-level
**A premium, founder-led AI software studio.** Full stop. Not "Gulf software studio," not "ZATCA compliance software," not any single product or region. The three Gulf products are proof of capability — evidence the studio can build regulated, high-stakes, real-world systems — not the identity itself.

## The split that fixes this
Keep two layers strictly separate, and never let one leak into the other:

1. **Brand layer (site-wide, all visitors, all regions):** premium, minimal, global. Talks about capability, craft, and outcomes in the abstract — never names a single region as the audience.
2. **Proof layer (a dedicated, secondary part of the site — `/work`, one line per product):** where ZATCA, SanadOS, and TehsilOS actually get named, briefly, as evidence. A visitor has to go looking for the detail; it never announces itself on the homepage hero, the nav labels, or the meta description.

This is exactly how Apple's homepage works: you don't get product-line detail until you click into a product page. The homepage sells the company's taste and capability, not a spec sheet.

## What this changes concretely
- Homepage hero: no product names, no region names. Capability and craft only.
- Nav: `Work`, `Studio`, `Contact` — not `Products` with three sub-items pushed to the top level.
- `/work`: the three products (and PulseKart/OrderFlow/Veloria Vault) live here as a portfolio, shown, not explained at length. One line of context per piece, not a pitch.
- No page, card, or CTA repeats "ZATCA" or "Gulf" more than once. If a concept needs saying twice on one page, it means the page is over-written, not that the point needs reinforcing.

## Where "Gulf-first" still correctly lives: outreach only
The Outreach Strategy documents (Saudi-first, then Oman) remain correct and unchanged — that's who gets emailed first, not what the website says. A cold email to a Saudi finance lead can and should reference ZATCA directly, because that's a targeted, private message to a qualified buyer. The public website is a different surface with a different, universal audience, and it's judged by a different standard.

## Tone standard going forward
Every piece of copy from here on gets tested against one question: *would this sentence survive on a page an Apple or Rolex-caliber visitor would respect?* If a sentence explains, justifies, or reassures ("we're founder-led, which means direct access, faster iteration...") it's cut or reduced to a fragment. Confidence is shown by what's left out, not what's added.

## Caveats
- This doesn't change the underlying products, pilots, or outreach targets — only how much of that shows up on the public-facing brand surface, and where.
- TehsilOS scope confirmation is still outstanding and still blocks its own proof-layer entry.
